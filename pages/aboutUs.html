About Us .

Pietential, LLC is headquartered in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania “The birthplace of America” and was founded by John Starling and Henry Hank Mitchell in 2019, and we're on a mission to bring our 'Life Balance Realization System' to the world, empowering people and organizations to understand and realize their full potential. And, as a company with a double bottom line we are committed to being a democratizing force for people and communities around the world that can't afford professional coaching.

John Starling

Chief Executive Officer

John Starling has dedicated his life to helping people define and achieve what's important to them and is the creator of "Pietential".
Since 1999 he's owned and operated several management consulting firms and has been a trusted business advisor and coach to nearly 400 companies (from start-ups to Fortune 50 companies) and countless individuals.
He is currently in private practice as a professional coach (Starling Growth Advisory) and leads CEO peer advisory groups for Learner Leader Cohorts.
In 2010 he founded a first of its kind MMA (Mentoring & Martial Arts) school for low-income, at-risk youth in Baltimore.
In 2016 he founded a girls' soccer club for elementary-aged school girls in South Korea who had no opportunity to play organized sports in their schools due to institutional and cultural gender discrimination.
A creative at his core, John is an Americana singer-songwriter and the author of a children's book about being okay with being yourself, "Flight of the Platypus"—a story of self-discovery.

Pooja Semwal

Director of Operations

Pooja Semwal is trained as a professional social worker and is a recipient of a prestigious Indian educational honor, a gold medal in Bachelor of Social Work.
With two master's degrees, one in Social Work and the other in Rural Development, she particularly likes solving people-oriented issues and helping individuals find workable and sustainable solutions. She was a frontline worker with many organizations in her academic field and did multiple research projects, including ‘A Study of Psycho-Social and Economic Problems of Men Affected by Domestic Violence.’ Pooja also worked full-time as a fundraiser trainer for MSF (Doctors Without Borders).
In 2018, after years of experiencing different ailments, she was finally diagnosed with an autoimmune disorder called Lupus. This changed her approach, but not her impact.
Pooja joined Pietential to continue to make a difference through her initiative, writing, research, advocacy, and management skills. As Director of Operations at Pietential, she oversees a wide range of strategic initiatives and keeps the team and processes streamlined and organized.
